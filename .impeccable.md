# Impeccable Design Context — Scaler PGPMT

## Design Context

### Users
Working professionals, 27–38. Self-funded. Mid-career managers, senior ICs, founders-adjacent. Browsing on desktop primarily, occasionally mobile. Considering this alongside traditional MBA programs. The core tension: "Can I justify spending ₹X lakh on this vs a traditional degree?"

### Brand Personality
Sharp. Forward. Institutional-but-not-stuffy. Three words: **ambitious, precise, inevitable**.

The program is about AI + Business leadership. The page should feel like the intersection of a top-tier B-school and a fast-moving tech company — not generic edtech.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Light mode. Professionals research this during working hours on bright screens.
- **Tone:** Modern tech company energy — confident, data-forward, no fluff. Like if Stripe built a program brochure.
- **NOT:** Generic edtech (stock photos, pastel gradients, endless icon grids). NOT a typical MBA brochure (stuffy serif, parchment tones). NOT AI-slop dark mode with glow effects.
- **Visual reference feel:** Linear.app, Vercel, Stripe — precise layouts, strong typographic hierarchy, data treated as design elements.
- **Colors:** Scaler blue `#0055FF` as accent. Navy `#011845` as deep anchor. Warm-tinted off-whites for surfaces. Sharp, not soft.
- **Corners:** Zero radius. Everything is squared. Non-negotiable — it's part of Scaler's brand DNA.
- **Font pairing:** Clash Grotesk (display/headings) + body font TBD — avoid the reflex list.

### Design Principles
1. **Precision over decoration** — every element earns its place. No filler icons, no gratuitous cards.
2. **Data is the hero** — numbers, outcomes, ROI signals are design elements, not footnotes.
3. **Confidence without noise** — strong hierarchy, generous whitespace, no visual screaming.
4. **Trust through specificity** — vague promises feel cheap. Specific stats, named alumni, real outcomes.
5. **Forward motion** — layout should feel like momentum, not a brochure you flip through.
